What locals ask

Tampa Bay electrical FAQ

A few of the questions homeowners and business owners around Tampa and St. Petersburg ask when planning electrical work.

Do I really need a permit for electrical work in Tampa Bay?
Most substantial electrical work in Tampa and St. Petersburg—like panel replacements, new circuits, service upgrades and remodels—requires a permit and inspection. Smaller repairs may not, but we always follow local code requirements so your work passes inspection now and during future home sales.
How do I know if my electrical panel should be replaced?
Warning signs include frequent breaker trips, rust or heat damage, buzzing sounds, and panels from obsolete or recalled manufacturers. During a visit we can inspect your panel, check load calculations and give you clear recommendations instead of guessing.
Can you install EV charging in a townhouse or condo?
In many cases, yes. We review your panel capacity, parking layout and HOA or building rules, then propose charging options that fit your situation—from dedicated garage circuits to shared charging plans where allowed.
What about storms and lightning along the Gulf Coast?
Tampa Bay sees frequent lightning, so we focus on proper grounding and bonding, whole-home surge protection and correct installation of sensitive electronics. These steps help protect your equipment and can reduce nuisance failures after storms.
